## Two kinds of explanation

System dynamics prefers **endogenous** explanations — behavior generated by the model's own feedback structure. A population grows because births exceed deaths. Inventory oscillates because delayed ordering creates overshoot. The causes are internal.

But sometimes you need **exogenous** inputs — forces that come from outside the model's feedback structure:

- A policy intervention at a specific time
- Seasonal demand patterns
- A one-time shock (natural disaster, market crash)
- Historical data you want the model to track

Exogenous inputs are not inherently bad, but they should be used deliberately. If an input *should* respond to system state, it belongs inside the model as endogenous structure. If it truly comes from outside — a regulation imposed by government, a weather pattern — then an exogenous input is appropriate.

The rule of thumb: **use exogenous inputs sparingly**. Too many suggest your model boundary is too narrow.
