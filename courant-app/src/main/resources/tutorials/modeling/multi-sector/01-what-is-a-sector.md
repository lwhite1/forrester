## Major subsystems in large models

A **sector** is a major subsystem in a large model. Each sector has its own stocks and flows, but sectors interact through shared variables.

## Examples by domain

**Business model:**
- Production -- capacity, work in process, output rate
- Marketing -- customer awareness, advertising, sales
- Finance -- revenue, costs, cash flow
- Human Resources -- workforce, hiring, training

**Urban model:**
- Housing -- units, construction, demolition
- Transportation -- roads, congestion, commute time
- Employment -- jobs, labor force, unemployment

**Ecological model:**
- Population -- births, deaths, migration
- Resources -- harvest, regeneration, depletion
- Pollution -- emissions, cleanup, accumulation

## Why sectors matter

No single sector explains system behavior on its own. The interactions between sectors create emergent dynamics -- oscillation, overshoot, policy resistance -- that you can't see by analyzing subsystems in isolation.
