## What you learned

- **Sectors** -- major subsystems with their own stocks, flows, and internal feedback
- **Decomposition** -- divide complex systems into sectors with clear functions, internal dynamics, and well-defined interfaces
- **Coupling** -- sectors interact through shared variables that create system-wide feedback loops
- **Modules** -- implement sectors as modules with input and output bindings for clean interfaces
- **Incremental construction** -- build and test sectors individually, then connect them one at a time

## Key insight

Multi-sector models reveal **emergent behavior** -- dynamics that arise from the interaction between sectors but are invisible when analyzing any single sector alone. Price oscillation, supply chain bullwhip effects, and urban sprawl are all emergent phenomena that require multi-sector models to understand.
