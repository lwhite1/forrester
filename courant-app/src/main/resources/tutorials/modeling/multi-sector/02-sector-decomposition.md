## Dividing a system into manageable pieces

**Sector decomposition** is the art of dividing a complex system into subsystems that are individually understandable and collectively complete.

## Criteria for good sectors

Each sector should have:

- **A clear function** -- it represents a coherent subsystem (production, finance, population)
- **Internal feedback loops** -- it has its own dynamics, not just pass-through calculations
- **Well-defined interfaces** -- the connections to other sectors are explicit and few

## The process

1. **Start with causal loop diagrams** -- identify the major feedback loops in the system
2. **Group related loops** -- loops that share stocks and operate on similar timescales belong in the same sector
3. **Define boundaries** -- decide which variables are internal to each sector and which cross sector boundaries
4. **Name the interfaces** -- list the variables that flow between sectors

## How many sectors?

A model with 3-6 sectors is typical. Fewer than 3 and you haven't decomposed enough. More than 8-10 and the sector map itself becomes hard to understand. Each sector should contain roughly 10-30 elements.
