## Not everything is a straight line

Many real-world relationships are non-linear:

- **Productivity vs. workload** — output rises with effort, but adding the 10th hour of work doesn't produce as much as the 2nd (diminishing returns)
- **Price vs. demand** — small price increases barely affect sales, until a threshold where demand collapses (elastic then inelastic)
- **Motivation vs. reward** — the first bonus matters a lot, the fifth much less

Linear equations force you to pick a single slope. Reality bends. A straight line between "no effort" and "maximum effort" misses the curve entirely.

**Table functions** (also called lookup tables or graphical functions) let you draw the shape of the relationship instead of writing an equation for it. You supply a set of (x, y) points, and the simulator interpolates between them.

This tutorial shows you how to create and use table functions to capture diminishing returns, saturation, and S-shaped responses.
