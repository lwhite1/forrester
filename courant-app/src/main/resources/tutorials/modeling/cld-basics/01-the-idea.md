## Map the causal structure of urban traffic congestion

Before building a quantitative simulation, system dynamics practitioners sketch a **Causal Loop Diagram (CLD)** — a map of cause-and-effect relationships that reveals the feedback structure driving system behavior.

In this tutorial you will diagram a familiar problem: **traffic congestion**. The story goes like this:

- More **Cars on Road** cause greater **Congestion**
- Greater Congestion increases **Travel Time**
- Longer Travel Time makes people choose **Public Transit**
- More Public Transit means fewer Cars on Road

But there is a second loop: when congestion rises, the city builds more roads, which *initially* reduces congestion — but eventually attracts more drivers.

By the end, you'll see how two interlocking feedback loops explain why building more roads does not solve congestion.
