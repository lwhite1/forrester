## What you learned

- **CLD variables** — qualitative placeholders that capture causal relationships without equations or units
- **Causal links** — directed arrows showing cause-and-effect
- **Polarity** — whether a cause increases (+) or decreases (−) its effect
- **Balancing loops (B)** — odd number of negative links; goal-seeking behavior
- **Reinforcing loops (R)** — even number of negative links (or all positive); self-amplifying behavior
- **Induced demand** — how building capacity can attract new usage, undermining the intended fix

## When to use a CLD

- At the start of a project, to map out the system before formalizing equations
- For stakeholder communication — CLDs are accessible to non-modelers
- To identify counter-intuitive feedback before investing in simulation detail
