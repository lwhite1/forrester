## Identify Feedback Loops

Click the **Loops** toggle button in the toolbar to activate loop highlighting. The loop navigator bar appears at the bottom.

Your diagram contains two feedback loops:

**Loop 1 — Balancing (B): The Transit Loop**
  Cars on Road `+`→ Congestion `+`→ Travel Time `+`→ Public Transit Use `−`→ Cars on Road

Count the negative links: there is **one** negative link, making this a **balancing loop (B)**. Balancing loops are goal-seeking — this loop works to reduce congestion by pushing people to transit.

**Loop 2 — Balancing (B): The Road Building Loop**
  Congestion `+`→ Road Construction → Road Capacity `−`→ Congestion

(Note: we have not drawn the Road Construction → Road Capacity link yet. Add it now with polarity **+** — more construction leads to more capacity.)

This loop also has one negative link — another **balancing loop**. But here is the key insight: this loop has a *long delay* between Road Construction and Road Capacity. And once capacity appears, it attracts new drivers — a phenomenon called **induced demand**.

Use the **◀** and **▶** buttons (or press `[` and `]`) to step through each loop and see it highlighted on the diagram.
