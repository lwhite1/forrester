## Create a baby boom

Double the birth rate and re-run. Trace the bulge as it moves through the chain:

- Children swell immediately
- Adults swell 15 years later
- Elderly swell 65 years later

The wave takes a lifetime to pass through. This is why demographic shifts play out over decades, not years.

## Reduce elderly mortality

Cut the elderly death rate in half. What happens to the age distribution over time? The elderly cohort grows, shifting the population's center of gravity upward. This is the demographic transition many countries face today.

## Add a co-flow

Try adding a co-flow for "years of education":

1. Create three new stocks paralleling the cohort stocks
2. Add education accumulation during childhood (e.g., 1 year of education per year)
3. Mirror the aging flows so education transfers with the population

Run the simulation. Does average education change as the population ages? What happens to workforce education 20 years after an education reform?
