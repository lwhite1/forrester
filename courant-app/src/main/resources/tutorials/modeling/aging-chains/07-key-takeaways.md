## What you learned

- **Aging chains** model stage progression with a series of stocks connected by flows
- **Cohorts** represent groups at each stage; residence time controls the duration of each stage
- **Conservation** means entities aren't created or destroyed by aging — they move between stages
- **Co-flows** track attributes (education, health, wealth) alongside the main population
- **Conveyors** provide fixed-time delays for deterministic processes

## Behavior modes seen

- Wave propagation (a baby boom moving through cohorts)
- Demographic transition (aging population structure)
- Delayed attribute effects (education reform reaching the workforce)

## Key insight

Aging chains reveal the **momentum** in demographic and stage-structured systems. Even if birth rates change today, the population structure won't fully adjust for decades — the existing cohorts must age through the chain. This momentum makes early intervention crucial and late intervention ineffective.
