## Entities that progress through stages

Many systems involve entities that move through stages over time. People age from children to adults to elderly. Products move from new to mature to obsolete. Projects advance from design to development to testing.

A single stock can't capture these stages. A stock called "Population" tells you how many people exist, but not how many are children, working-age adults, or retirees. Those distinctions matter — they determine school enrollment, labor supply, and pension costs.

You need a **chain** of stocks, each representing a **cohort** that ages into the next. This is an **aging chain** — one of the most important structures in system dynamics.

By the end, you'll answer: *How does a baby boom propagate through an age-structured population over decades?*
