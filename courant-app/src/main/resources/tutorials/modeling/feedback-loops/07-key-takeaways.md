## What you learned

- **Reinforcing feedback** — infection spreads exponentially when susceptible people are plentiful
- **Balancing feedback** — recovery depletes the infectious pool, and depletion of susceptibles slows transmission
- **S-shaped growth** — the transition from reinforcing to balancing dominance produces a characteristic sigmoid curve
- **Multi-stock models** — material (people) flows between stocks conserving the total population
- **R₀** — the basic reproduction number determines whether the epidemic grows (R₀ > 1) or dies out (R₀ < 1)

## Behavior modes seen

- Exponential growth (early infection)
- Goal-seeking (recovery approaching total population)
- S-shaped growth (susceptible depletion curve)
