## A causal story about feedback

The **dynamic hypothesis** is a causal story explaining why the reference mode occurs. It identifies the feedback loops, delays, and nonlinearities that generate the problematic behavior.

## Why "dynamic"?

The hypothesis is "dynamic" because it explains behavior through the system's **feedback structure**, not through external events. Instead of "sales declined because of the recession," a dynamic hypothesis says "sales declined because the reinforcing loop between marketing spend and revenue weakened when market saturation reduced the effectiveness of advertising."

## How to develop one

1. **Identify feedback loops** -- what reinforcing loops drive growth? What balancing loops create limits or resistance?
2. **Identify delays** -- where does information or material take time to flow?
3. **Express as a CLD** -- draw a causal loop diagram showing the major loops and their polarity
4. **Map to the reference mode** -- which loop dominates in each phase of the behavior? Reinforcing loops explain growth; balancing loops explain goal-seeking; delayed balancing loops explain oscillation.

## From CLD to stock-and-flow

The CLD captures the causal logic. The next step is formalizing it as a stock-and-flow model with equations, which is the formulation stage.
