## A systematic approach

System dynamics modeling isn't just "build and run." It follows a systematic process with five stages:

1. **Problem articulation** -- define the scope and the question
2. **Dynamic hypothesis** -- develop a causal explanation
3. **Formulation** -- translate into a running simulation
4. **Testing** -- verify structure and behavior
5. **Policy design** -- explore interventions

Each stage builds on the previous. The process is iterative -- testing may reveal problems that send you back to reformulate equations or even rethink the dynamic hypothesis.

This process was codified by Sterman (2000) and is the standard approach in the field. Skipping stages leads to models that are technically functional but fail to provide insight.

This tutorial walks through each stage, with emphasis on the two that beginners most often skip: problem articulation and dynamic hypothesis.
