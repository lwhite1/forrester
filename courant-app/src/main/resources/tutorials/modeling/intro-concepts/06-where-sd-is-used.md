## Real-world applications

System Dynamics has been applied for over 60 years across a wide range of domains. It is most useful when problems involve **feedback**, **delays**, and **accumulation** — which turns out to be most important problems.

## Notable domains

- **Business strategy** — modeling market growth, product adoption, and competitive dynamics
- **Public health** — epidemic modeling (SIR/SEIR), healthcare capacity planning, disease prevention policy
- **Environment & climate** — resource depletion, pollution accumulation, climate policy evaluation
- **Supply chains** — inventory management, the bullwhip effect, production planning
- **Urban planning** — housing, transportation, land use, and infrastructure investment
- **Project management** — schedule pressure, rework cycles, and staffing decisions
- **Energy** — transitions between fuel sources, grid capacity, investment delays

## Landmark works

- **Industrial Dynamics** (Forrester, 1961) — the founding text; applied SD to corporate supply chains
- **Urban Dynamics** (Forrester, 1969) — showed how well-intentioned urban policies can backfire due to feedback
- **The Limits to Growth** (Meadows et al., 1972) — modeled global resource use and population; one of the most influential SD studies ever published

## The common thread

In every case, the system's behavior was surprising or counter-intuitive when viewed informally — but understandable once the feedback structure was made explicit. That is the value of System Dynamics.
