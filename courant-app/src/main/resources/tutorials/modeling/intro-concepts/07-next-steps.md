## What you learned

- **Mental models** are implicit and untestable; SD makes them explicit and simulatable
- **Reinforcing feedback** amplifies change (exponential growth, snowball effects)
- **Balancing feedback** resists change and seeks a goal (thermostats, homeostasis)
- **Stocks** accumulate over time and create inertia and memory
- **Flows** are the only way to change stocks, measured as rates per unit time
- **The endogenous view** explains behavior from within the system, not from external events
- SD is used wherever feedback, delays, and accumulation drive behavior

## Build your first model

You now understand the philosophy behind System Dynamics. In the next tutorial, you'll put these ideas into practice by building a **coffee cooling model** — a cup of hot coffee cooling to room temperature.

You'll place a stock, connect a flow, write equations, run a simulation, and experiment with parameters. You'll see balancing feedback in action as the cooling rate slows down the closer the coffee gets to room temperature.
