## How stocks influence each other

Two stocks are **coupled** when one stock's flow depends on the other stock's value. Neither stock can be understood in isolation.

Consider predators and prey:

- **Prey birth rate** depends on prey population (reinforcing loop -- more prey, more births).
- **Prey death rate** depends on *both* prey and predator populations (more predators means more prey killed).
- **Predator birth rate** depends on prey caught (predators need food to reproduce).
- **Predator death rate** depends on predator population (natural mortality).

This creates **mutual feedback**. Prey feed predator growth; predators suppress prey. The result is oscillation -- a behavior mode that cannot emerge from a single stock with simple feedback.

## The key difference

- **Single stock**: behavior is monotonic (growth, decay, or goal-seeking).
- **Coupled stocks**: behavior can oscillate, with each stock's peak and trough offset in time.
