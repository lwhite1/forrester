## What you learned

- **Coupled stocks** -- when one stock's flows depend on another stock's value, the two are coupled. This creates mutual feedback.
- **Oscillation from coupling** -- predator-prey dynamics produce sustained oscillation because each stock's growth drives the other's decline, with a time lag.
- **Cascading flows** -- stocks connected in series form chains (like SIR). Material is conserved as it moves through the pipeline.
- **System boundaries** -- the boundary separates endogenous dynamics from exogenous assumptions. Drawing it wrong hides feedback or adds noise.

## Behavior modes seen

- Sustained oscillation (classic Lotka-Volterra)
- Damped oscillation (with carrying capacity)
- Phase-shifted cycles (predator peaks lag prey peaks)

## Key insight

Oscillation in multi-stock systems arises from coupling and delay. The predator population responds to prey with a lag -- by the time predators peak, prey has already crashed. Understanding this lag is the key to understanding why coupled systems oscillate.
