## Real systems have multiple interacting stocks

So far, each tutorial has focused on a single stock. But real systems rarely work that way.

- A **population** has births and deaths, but also predators and food supply.
- An **economy** has capital, labor, and output stocks influencing each other.
- An **ecosystem** has prey and predators whose fates are intertwined.

When one stock's flows depend on another stock's value, you get **coupled dynamics** -- behavior that no single stock could produce alone. A lone population stock grows or decays exponentially. Two coupled population stocks can oscillate, stabilize, or crash together.

This tutorial explores what happens when stocks interact. By the end, you'll answer: *Why do predator and prey populations oscillate, and what controls the pattern?*
